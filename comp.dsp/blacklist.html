   Welcome to the comp.dsp blacklist  

The blacklist database contains the names of people prone to posting personal attacks and libelous remarks, using abusive or threatening rhetoric or deceiving and manipulating subscribers to the comp.dsp newsgroup. That is to say -- people prone to flaming, spamming and trolling.

New subscribers and other people who read comp.dsp infrequently are encouraged to consult the blacklist database before responding to any posting in the comp.dsp newsgroup.

Responding to anyone on the blacklist could have very unpleasant consequences.

These people are part of a tiny but very vocal clique of young programmers who know just enough about DSP to be really dangerous. They pose as friendly experts offering free consulting services but they are more interested in humiliating or exploiting new or unwary subscribers to the comp.dsp newsgroup. Beware of advice from people who have no credentials or people who want to sell you software or shareware.

Do not respond if one of these people flames you. You will only draw flames from all the other members of the clique. Just ignore them. It drives them crazy. Eventually, they will all end up on this blacklist and in everyone's killfile.

Don't flame other people. Just put their names in your killfile if you can't ignore them. If you lose control and flame someone in a fit of rage, cancel your posting immediately. Otherwise, your name may end up on this blacklist and in everyone's killfile.



Definitions for flaming, spamming and trolling can be found in the Free On-Line Dictionary of Computing.
